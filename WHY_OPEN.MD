# Why VeriForge Is Open

Verification only works if it is **trusted**.

Trust in AI systems does not come from proprietary claims or closed implementations. It comes from:
- Shared definitions
- Transparent processes
- Reproducible controls
- Independent scrutiny

VeriForge is open because decision verification must be **inspectable**.

---

## Why openness matters

AI systems are increasingly used in regulated, insured, and mission-critical environments. In these contexts:

- Regulators require clarity, not black boxes
- Insurers require consistent semantics and evidence
- Enterprises require defensible processes
- Users require accountability

A closed verification framework undermines trust at every level.

---

## What is open

VeriForge is open in its:
- Decision tier definitions
- Failure classification taxonomy
- Verification semantics and protocol
- Reference specifications

These elements benefit from public review and shared understanding.

---

## What is not required to be open

Openness does not require that every implementation be open.

Organizations may choose to:
- Build proprietary gateways
- Offer managed verification services
- Integrate commercial tooling

VeriForge defines **what must happen**, not **how it must be implemented**.
